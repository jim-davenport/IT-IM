What is the IT/Interactive Media Program?

The IT/Interactive Media program at Bridgeport High School gives students a hands‑on, creative, and career‑focused introduction to the world of technology. Whether students are interested in computers, design, programming, or digital media, this program builds real‑world skills that prepare them for college, technical training, or entry‑level industry certifications.

Students explore a wide range of technology fields through engaging, project‑based coursework, collaborative learning, and practical problem‑solving. From building PCs to designing websites and producing multimedia content, the program encourages students to think creatively and work like true IT professionals.

Program Highlights

Hands-On Learning

Students gain practical experience with cutting-edge technology and industry-standard tools.

Design & Development

Learn web design, graphic design, and software development from foundational to advanced levels.

Career Ready

Earn industry certifications and gain skills that lead directly to career opportunities.

Why Choose This Program?

The IT/Interactive Media program is designed to be inclusive and accessible to all students, regardless of their prior experience with technology. It provides a supportive environment where students can explore their interests, develop new skills, and discover potential career paths in the ever‑evolving tech industry.

By participating in this program, students gain valuable technical skills, enhance their creativity, and build confidence in their abilities. They also have opportunities to earn industry‑recognized certifications that can give them a head start in the job market or higher education.
